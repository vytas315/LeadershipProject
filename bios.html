Ari Studnitzer

Mr. Studnitzer, a Booth MBA graduate and Managing Director at CME Group, currently leads a small team of 3 direct reports, and indirectly leads a large-scale change effort enlisting the help of hundreds of people spanning multiple departments - without formal authority. He combines the change management methods described in “Tipping Point Leadership” (the HBR article) and “Give and Take” (a book by Adam Grant) with disruptive innovation (ala Clayton Christensen) to create his own unique brand of leadership steeped in Booth MBA teachings.
Why we selected Ari
We chose Ari as one of our interviewees to provide the perspective of someone currently embroiled in a battle for leading change in a successful competitive company that, with 55% margins, does not necessarily see the need to change. In this situation, Ari represents someone who has to fight for change to take hold, along the way overcoming politically driven gatekeepers.

Laura Truax

Rev. Laura Sumner Truax is the senior pastor at LaSalle Street Church, a non-denominational church in downtown Chicago with a long tradition of uniting individual faith in Christ with God's call for justice and compassion lived out in the world.

Laura is the author of Undone:When coming apart puts you back together, published by InterVarsity Press August 2013.

Rev. Truax holds a Master’s in Pastoral Studies with an emphasis in Spirituality and a Master’s of Divinity degree and serves as a teaching pastor for the University of Chicago and for World Vision International.
Why we selected Laura
We selected Laura because our theme was leading without formal authority. As the pastor of a church, Laura’s role is leading a congregation of people who are not compelled to come. There are many churches, so the success of a non-denominational church like LaSalle is directly tied to the quality of leadership and the pastor’s ability to foster active leaders and followers within the congregation.

Tom Behringer

Tom Behringer is a Senior Vice President at Sargent & Lundy LLC, an energy consulting firm located in Chicago. Tom has worked at Sargent & Lundy LLC his entire career. As a consultant for the company he has worked many different roles in the nuclear energy side of the company. Tom’s current role is the Exelon project team lead, where he consulting efforts on all of Exelon’s 10 nuclear power plants – Exelon’s fleet accounts for 25% of all US nuclear generation. Recent projects have included power uprates, where plants extend operating capacity, and Fukushima disaster related modifications. Tom has a bachelors degree in civil engineering from the University of Cincinnati and masters in civil engineering from the University of Illinois at Chicago.
Why we selected Tom
Tom was selected because he offered a unique perspective as a consultant to a very rigid, hierarchical industry: power generation. We felt that his insights into how best to lead a company that is at the mercy of the fast moving and demanding power generation industry would give us invaluable insight into some best practices for leadership. In addition to this, Tom is very well respected within his firm and it was felt he would be capable and willing to give honest but useful material on how to lead a unique organization.

Mike Meyer

Mike Meyer is a director at McMaster-Carr Supply Company. He works in the Systems department where under his guide teams of developers publish software for the rest of the company. Mike has worked at McMaster for over 15 years and has spent time at three of their five branches. In his current role, he is responsible for Business Systems, the component of the Systems department that manages the department’s relationship with the rest of the company. He has been integral to the development of a thread of work called Customer Projects. It is a series of projects that will unfold over the next 10 years.
Why we selected Mike
Software produced by Systems is not only used by other departments, but is also dependent on those departments for implementation as well as content and design guidance. In his current role, Mike navigates these departmental challenges where he often works with his peers to build support for the work Systems is doing.

Deb Clapp

Deb Clapp is the Executive Director for the League of Chicago Theatres, a nonprofit organization serving Chicagoland’s theatres and arts companies. During Deb’s tenure, the League has increased its membership and scope of programming and raised the visibility of Chicago theatre. It has also created successful initiatives such as Hottix and Chicago Theatres Week. Deb has guest lectured at Columbia College Chicago; DePaul University; and Florida State University. She has served as a grant panelist for the City of Chicago, Department of Cultural Affairs, City Arts Grants; and the Missouri Arts Council. She also served on the judges panel for the National Women’s Hall of Fame in 2012. Deb serves on the board of the Network of Ensemble Theatres.
Why we selected Deb
Deb leads with informal authority on a daily basis. In her line of work, she is acutely aware of the costs and benefits of informal leadership, as well as instances of its dangers or limitations. Given her years of leadership and consulting experience in the nonprofit industry, we selected Deb because we feel her experience will give us practical, meso-level insights.
